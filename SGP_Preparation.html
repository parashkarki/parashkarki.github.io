💡 Section 03

Tips & Tricks

Read these before anything else. These tips will change how you approach the whole interview.

😌

You Don't Need To Be Perfect — You Just Need This Script

The SGP interview is not a test of intelligence. Even if your Korean is basic, even if your hands shake a little — if you memorize the 25 sentences in the Script section and show up calm and respectful, you WILL be selected. Hundreds of international students pass this every year with just a simple script. The interviewers are not trying to trick you. They just want to know: "Can I rely on this person?"

1
Mindset

Script = More Than Enough

You do not need to improvise. Memorize the 25 sentences and say them calmly. Simple and clear always beats long and impressive.

2
Action

Speak Slowly On Purpose

Nervous people always speak fast. Slow Korean signals confidence and respect. A 2-second pause before answering looks like thinking — not panicking.

3
Secret

First 10 Seconds = 50% of Result

Walk in straight, say 안녕하세요 immediately, bow ~15°, gentle smile. The interviewer decides half their feeling before you even sit down.

4
Avoid

Don't Over-Apologize for Korean

Saying "죄송합니다 한국어 못해요" more than once is a weakness signal. Say it once, then move forward. Show growth: "I will improve."

5
Action

Practice Out Loud — Not Just Reading

Reading silently doesn't prepare your mouth. Say each sentence out loud 5 times. Record yourself. Listen back. Fix what sounds unnatural.

6
Mindset

They WANT You To Succeed

SGP needs students. The interviewer is hoping you'll say the right things. Think of it as a conversation, not an exam. They're rooting for you.

7
Secret

"Early Morning" Is A Filter Question

Hesitation = fail. "Maybe" = fail. 네, 가능합니다! with a confident smile = you move ahead of most candidates instantly.

8
Action

Arrive 10–15 Minutes Early

Being early says "I take this seriously" before you even speak. SGP is about discipline — show it starts before the room.

9
Avoid

Don't Sound Robotic

Memorized scripts sound fake in a flat tone. Add small natural pauses. Nod. Breathe. Make it feel like a real conversation.

10
Secret

The Interview Starts At The Door

Staff may watch how you arrive and greet the receptionist. Be respectful everywhere — not just inside the interview room.

11
Mindset

They Can't See Your Heartbeat

Your heart may race — that's normal. They only see posture, eye contact, tone. Sit straight + speak slowly = you look calm.

12
Action

Exit With 감사합니다 + Bow

Stand, say 감사합니다, bow ~20°, walk out calmly. A polite exit can turn a "maybe" into a "yes." Never rush out.

🎯 Section 06

Interview Strategy

How to physically act in the room. Follow these 6 steps and you'll look calm and professional — even if you feel nervous inside.

1

Enter → Greet Immediately

Walk in confidently. The moment you cross the door, say "안녕하세요" and bow ~15°. Don't wait. Don't hesitate. This single action sets the entire tone.

2

Sit Straight, Don't Fidget

Feet flat on floor. Hands on thighs or table. Don't cross arms. No touching your face. No phone. Stillness = confidence.

3

Speak Slowly & Clearly

Slow Korean = confident Korean. Pause before answering — 2 seconds is fine and looks like thoughtfulness. Never rush to fill silence.

4

Gentle, Steady Eye Contact

Look at the interviewer while speaking — not a stare, a calm respectful gaze. Look away briefly when thinking, return when speaking.

5

Relaxed Smile — Not a Nervous Grin

A calm slight smile says: "I am comfortable. I want to be here." Too wide = nervous. No expression = cold. Find the natural middle.

6

Exit With Dignity

When done — stand up, say "감사합니다" clearly, bow ~20°, walk out calmly. The last image they see is as important as the first.

You Are NOT Trying To Impress.
You Are Trying To Look Reliable.

Attitude + eye contact + confidence + simple clear answers.
That is what gets you selected — not Korean fluency, not intelligence.
